LOOP MODE — Workflow Guide

ILLPAD48 V2 · visual manual for players

What is LOOP mode

LOOP mode turns a bank into a percussion looper. You get three dedicated pads on the layout — REC, PLAY/STOP, CLEAR — plus the 45 remaining pads which play drum sounds (fixed GM mapping, no scale, no aftertouch).

The workflow is simple: record a pattern, let it loop, overdub, clear when done. A LOOP bank stays alive in the background when you switch to another bank — the groove keeps playing.

Up to 2 LOOP banks can exist at the same time. Each bank has its own independent loop and its own quantize setting. You can have one bank running a tight quantized groove and another bank as a free-feel looper, simultaneously.

The three quantize modes

Every LOOP bank has a quantize setting (Tool 4 in setup mode). It controls how the six "when does the transition fire?" moments behave: start record, close record, start overdub, close overdub, play, stop.

FREE

Free loop

Everything fires the instant you tap. No snap, no wait. The loop you create is aligned on your own timing, not the master clock.

Feel → spontaneous, reactive, slightly floating. Good for ambient, experimental beats, solo play without a backing track.

LED: solid hot magenta-red while playing. No beat/bar pulsing (the loop has no master-clock-aligned grid to reveal).
BEAT

Snap to beat

Transitions wait for the next 1/4 note boundary before firing (up to ~1 beat delay). Your loop is locked to the master clock down to the beat.

Feel → snug, tight, groove-friendly. Good for layering over existing tracks, live jamming with a DAW.

LED: cooler magenta-violet base + beat/bar/wrap tick flashes (hierarchical boost). You see the 1/4 notes and bars passing.
BAR

Snap to bar

Transitions wait for the next downbeat of a 4-beat measure (up to ~4 beats delay). Your loop is locked to bar phrasing.

Feel → epic, arranged, song-structure. Good for intros/outros, bringing a loop in on the "1" of a new section, stopping at the end of a chorus.

LED: same base color as BEAT + the same tick flash hierarchy. What changes is the timing of quantized transitions, not the visual language.
How to change the mode: enter setup mode (rear button during boot), go to Tool 4, select a LOOP bank, press Up/Down to cycle Free → Beat → Bar. The setting persists across reboots.
Waiting quantize feedback: in BEAT/BAR mode, when you tap an action that must wait for a boundary, the LED switches to a rapid blink in the action's color (red for rec/stop, green for play) until the boundary is reached and the action fires. You always know your tap was registered — no more "did it see my press?" uncertainty.

CLEAR long-press OVERDUBBING PLAYING UNDO OVERDUB — always immediate. Discards only the events captured during the current overdub pass. The underlying loop keeps playing without interruption — no audible gap, no retrigger. Use this when you mess up an overdub and want to try again without losing the base loop. The 500 ms hold is the "human quantize".
CLEAR long-press any other (except EMPTY) EMPTY WIPE LOOP — always immediate. The loop is completely wiped when the LED ramp completes. Destructive, no undo. Quantize mode is ignored.
REC tap STOPPED — Ignored
P/S tap EMPTY or RECORDING — Ignored
About "trailing tails": when you stop playback, notes that were scheduled with shuffle, chaos, or a long gate time keep firing naturally until they finish. The loop stops cleanly but doesn't clip the tail of a sustained note. This is what shuffle/chaos sound natural.

Mid-bar 1, you tap PLAY on a previously recorded loop. The loop waits up to a full bar, then fires on the "1" of bar 2 — perfect for bringing an intro in at the right phrase boundary. Stopping works the same way in reverse: tap STOP mid-bar, the loop finishes the current bar, then stops on the next downbeat.

Edge cases and tips

Waiting for a boundary feels like doing nothing. Your tap was registered. In BEAT mode the wait is at most 1 beat (~500 ms at 120 BPM), in BAR mode up to 4 beats (~2 s at 120 BPM). Plan your taps slightly ahead. LED feedback in Phase 4 will show the "waiting" state visibly — for now, trust that the tap was seen.
No master clock? In BEAT or BAR mode without an external clock, the ILLPAD uses its internal tempo (pot R1, 10–260 BPM). The boundary logic works identically.
Two ways to undo a bad overdub pass:
• CLEAR long-press during OVERDUBBING → undo overdub: discards only the current pass, the underlying loop keeps playing seamlessly. You stay in PLAYING state, ready to try another overdub. No audible gap, no retrigger. This is the musical "oops, let me try that again" button.
• PLAY/STOP tap during OVERDUBBING → abort: the overdub is thrown away and playback stops (clean break). The original loop is untouched — tap PLAY/STOP again and it resumes from the top. Use this when you want to stop altogether, not just retry.
Background playback: when you switch to another bank, your LOOP keeps playing on its own MIDI channel. Up to 2 LOOP banks can play simultaneously, each with independent quantize, shuffle, and groove settings.
CLEAR is destructive outside OVERDUBBING. In PLAYING, STOPPED, or RECORDING state, a completed CLEAR long-press wipes the entire loop — there is no undo. The 500 ms hold is your safety: if you start the hold by accident, release within the LED ramp window to cancel. In OVERDUBBING state, CLEAR has a different, non-destructive meaning (undo overdub) — the base loop is safe.
Bank switch during recording is blocked. If you try to switch banks while a LOOP bank is in RECORDING or OVERDUBBING state, the switch is silently denied. Close the recording first (tap REC) or abort (PLAY/STOP for overdub).
